Medical Marijuana & CBD: Evidence of Statements!

When it comes to Medical Marijuana, there's been a rise in the curiosity surrounding the healing qualities of marijuana, which has resulted in more people using it for health purposes and the creation of programs tailored to each state's medical cannabis laws. Marijuana is legal in 33 states and Washington, D.C.

Part of the reason for the scarcity of studies involving humans is that cannabis is still classified as a schedule 1 substance by the US federal government. Even though some states in the US have legalized it, there are still numerous bureaucratic hurdles and legal challenges that prevent researchers from examining the potential health benefits of marijuana for people.

The goal of this project is to investigate and examine the specific uses of medical marijuana that have proven to be effective. This data set is obtained from Kaggle website.

As you can see the data set has several columns: health condition: The medical condition under consideration. alternate name: Alternate names for the medical condition, if any. health condition.1: A general category for the health condition. evidence score 0 = harmful \n1 = no / insufficient evidence\n6 = strong: A score representing the strength of the evidence for the efficacy of cannabis or CBD in treating the condition. A score of 0 indicates that cannabis or CBD is harmful, 1 indicates no or insufficient evidence, and 6 indicates strong evidence. popular interest: A measure of the popular interest in the condition and its treatment with cannabis or CBD. Number of citations on Google Scholar (2000-2017) search format: condition+cannabis: The number of citations on Google Scholar for articles related to the condition and cannabis. CBD: A column indicating whether CBD is involved in the treatment. simple English notes: A summary of the evidence in simple English. notes: Detailed notes on the evidence. link: A link to the source of the evidence. other International review board or metastudy: A link to another international review board or metastudy, if any. individual study: A link to an individual study, if any. main study source name: The name of the main study source. other sources: Other sources, if any.

Here are the health issues from the data set where the use of cannabis or CBD is thought to be detrimental, based on the evidence score (evidence_score = 0):

Adolescence: This situation is categorized in neuroscience. Using marijuana during the teenage period is thought to impact one's ability to control impulses, retain information, and focus. Asthma: This situation is categorized in the respiratory system. Smoking marijuana temporarily widens the airways, but soon leads to severe narrowing of the bronchi. Depression: This situation is categorized in mental health. Research shows that there's no proof that marijuana can alleviate depression. In fact, excessive use may even exacerbate depression. Fertility: This situation is categorized in reproduction. Marijuana use reduces the ability to conceive in both men and women. Schizophrenia: This situation is categorized in mental health. There is evidence that marijuana does not improve symptoms of psychosis and may even increase the chance of developing psychosis. Stroke: This situation is categorized in the cardiovascular system. Several case studies and large-scale studies suggest that using marijuana is linked to an increased risk of stroke. These examples show that the effects of cannabis or CBD are not consistent and can pose risks for some health conditions.

These factors suggest that while cannabis or CBD might not be universally advantageous and could pose risks for some health issues, it is generally not harmful and might offer benefits.

Observation:

Inadequate Strong Proof: The effectiveness of cannabis or CBD in treating various health issues is backed by only two conditions: Insomnia and Multiple Sclerosis (MS), with substantial evidence (evidence_score >= 5) for both.

Lack of or Weak Evidence: For the vast majority of health conditions included in the study, there is either a lack of evidence (evidence_score = 0) or weak evidence (evidence_score < 1) supporting the use of cannabis or CBD. Conditions such as Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), Alzheimers disease, and Autism fall into this category.

Negative Consequences: In certain health issues, the use of cannabis or CBD is deemed negative (evidence_score = 0). This encompasses conditions such as Adolescence, Asthma, and Depression.

Variation among Types: The evidence for the effectiveness or harm of cannabis or CBD in treating different types of health conditions is notably broad, with no definitive pattern of effectiveness or harm across all categories.

Popular Demand vs. Scientific Proof: There appears to be no direct link between public demand for cannabis or CBD and the robustness of the scientific evidence supporting their efficacy. Certain conditions highly sought after by the public show little scientific backing, while the opposite is also true.

CBD Use: The vast majority of conditions in the dataset do not involve CBD in their treatment, with only a few exceptions.

It's important to remember that this review is based on the data available up to 2017, and new studies may have been conducted since then. Moreover, the legal status of cannabis and CBD varies by location, which could impact their accessibility and use for medical purposes.
